Charbroiled Salmon

Description

This charbroiled salmon recipe is a delightful way to savor the rich and succulent flavors of this prized fish. Charbroiling imparts a smoky essence while maintaining the salmon's natural juiciness, resulting in a perfectly cooked and incredibly flavorful dish. The simple yet elegant combination of seasonings and a touch of lemon enhances the natural flavors of the salmon, making it a true crowd-pleaser. Whether you're hosting a dinner party or enjoying a cozy meal at home, this charbroiled salmon will impress your taste buds and leave you craving more.

Ingredients

  • Fresh salmon fillets
  • Olive oil
  • Lemon juice
  • Minced garlic
  • Dried dill
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Steps

  1. Preheat the grill to medium-high heat.
  2. Ensure the salmon fillets are clean and pat them dry.
  3. Brush both sides of the salmon fillets with olive oil.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together lemon juice, minced garlic, dried dill, salt, and black pepper to create the marinade.
  5. Generously brush the marinade over both sides of the salmon fillets.
  6. Carefully place the salmon fillets onto the grill grates, skin side down if applicable.
  7. Close the grill and cook the salmon for approximately 4-6 minutes per side, depending on the thickness of the fillets.
  8. Aim for an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C) for perfectly cooked salmon.
  9. Avoid flipping the salmon too frequently; allow each side to develop char and grill marks.
  10. Once cooked, transfer the salmon to a serving platter or individual plates.
  11. Garnish with fresh dill or a lemon wedge.
  12. Serve the charbroiled salmon with your choice of sides, such as roasted vegetables, rice pilaf, or a fresh salad.
  13. Enjoy the succulent and flavorful charbroiled salmon, celebrating its natural beauty and taste.
